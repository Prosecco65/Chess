Rules of chess!

Originating in India around the 6th century AD, chess came to Europe around the year 1000, thanks to the Arabs. Chess is played on an 8x8 board with a total of 64 squares on which 16 white pieces and 16 black pieces face off. The pieces are divided into 8 pawns, 2 rooks, 2 knights, 2 bishops, a king, each moving differently. The white pieces start the game. The pieces can eat the opponent's pieces except for the king, which cannot be eaten. The goal is to checkmate the king, that is, to block all possible moves. When the king is in checkmate he is obliged to move.
